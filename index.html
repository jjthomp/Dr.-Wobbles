Female Connector Preparation

  1. Cut your wires to the correct lengths (~ 6cm each).
  2. Strip a small section of insulation from each wire end.
  3. Crimp a connector pin onto each stripped wire end.
  4. For each connector housing, press one red and one black wire in until they click.

Soldering buttons, male connectors, and wires

  1. Solder the microcontroller to the microcontroller square on the PCB.
  2. Solder 1 x 2.0 mm connector to the BAT port on the PCB.
  3. Solder 2 x 2.5 mm connectors to the A2 and A3 ports on the PCB.
  4. Solder the black connector wires to the middle pin on the button.
  5. Solder the A2 red connector wire to the button pin labeled "a".
  6. Solder the A3 red connector wire to the button pin labeled "b".

Phase 3: Code & Connections

1. Loading the Software

Download the software folder from our GitHub: Software Folder. Before loading the software, set up the Arduino environment and add the microcontroller by following this guide: Arduino IDE Setup Guide. Then load the software onto the microcontroller before assembling the device.

2. Connecting Components

Plug each component into the correct port on the microcontroller:

  • Motor: Plug into Port A1
  • Button "a": Plug into Port A2
  • Button "b": Plug into Port A3
  • Battery: Plug into Port BAT

Phase 4: Final Assembly

Follow these steps in order. Refer to the images for exact placement.

1. Materials Overview

Lay out all your parts before starting: the two printed enclosure halves, circuit board, battery, motor, elastic bands, straps, and sticker.

2. Elastic Band Placement

Thread the elastic bands through the two slots on the back of the enclosure. Tie a knot on each end to hold them in place.

3. Battery Insertion

Place the battery into the lower compartment of the enclosure.

4. Motor Placement

Press the motor into its slot until it sits flat. Apply a small dot of hot glue or super glue around the base to hold it in place.

5. Circuit Board Placement

Slide the circuit board into the slot above the battery. Apply a small dot of hot glue or super glue to hold it in place.

6. Circuit Board Holder

Press the printed holder down over the circuit board to keep it in place.

7. Wire Connections

Plug each connector into its matching port on the circuit board. Make sure each connector clicks in fully.

8. Close the Enclosure

Line up the two halves of the enclosure and press them together. Check that no wires are caught in the seam. Twist to lock. Press the button firmly into the slot.

9. Strap Insertion

Thread each strap through the side loops, fold the end over, and sew it down.

10. Sticker Placement

Peel and apply the seal sticker to the front of the device.

Charging the Device

  1. 1 Remove the battery from the bottom of the device.
  2. 2 Plug the battery into the battery charger.
  3. 3 Plug the battery charger into a power outlet using a USB-C cable.
  4. 4 Allow the battery to charge for 60 minutes from full depletion.
  5. 5 Once fully charged, reinsert the battery into the bottom of the device and close the compartment.

Using the Device

  1. 1 Place the ice pack in the freezer for at least 2 hours prior to use.
  2. 2 Slot the ice pack into the elastic band on the device.
  3. 3 Place Dr. Wobbles directly on the intended injection site. If needed, secure the hook-and-loop straps around the patient's limb to hold it in place.
  4. 4 Hold in place for 30 seconds to allow the cold and vibration to take effect.
  5. 5 Slide the device a few centimetres away from the injection site, toward the center of the body, to expose the site for injection. Keep the device on and in place during the procedure.
  6. 6 Administer the injection.
  7. 7 After use, disinfect the device by spraying with 70% isopropyl alcohol or another appropriate clinical disinfectant and allow to air dry.
